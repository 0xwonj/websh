# NNNN — Title in imperative present tense

- **Status**: Proposed | Accepted | Superseded by NNNN | Deprecated
- **Date**: YYYY-MM-DD
- **Phase**: A | B | C | D | E | F | G | (cross-phase)

## Context

What forced this decision. The constraint, the conflict, the moment in execution when "the doc said X but evidence says Y."

Two to four sentences. Not a chronicle of how we got here.

## Decision

What we chose.

One paragraph. Direct, declarative. "We do X."

## Consequences

What this implies, both good and bad.

- Positive consequence — what improves.
- Negative consequence — what hurts or what we accept as cost.
- Follow-on — anything this enables or precludes for future work.

## Alternatives considered

- **Alternative A** — what it was, why we didn't pick it.
- **Alternative B** — what it was, why we didn't pick it.

Two to four alternatives. If only one was considered, the decision wasn't actually weighed and probably doesn't need an ADR.
